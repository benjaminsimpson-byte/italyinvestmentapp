Tax Guide

Italian & UK tax obligations for a UK higher-rate taxpayer letting a holiday property in Italy.

⚠️ Disclaimer: This is an educational guide only. Always consult a qualified Italian commercialista and UK tax advisor before making investment decisions.
UK tax band:
You pay 40% UK income tax; Italian 21% is credited → ~19% net additional to HMRC

🇮🇹 Italian Taxes on Rental Income

Cedolare Secca: 21%

A flat substitute tax replacing IRPEF (income tax) and registration tax on rental contracts. Applied to gross rental income — no expense deductions under this regime.

First holiday let property rate21%
2nd, 3rd, 4th property rate (since Jan 2025)26%
Is it optional?Yes — opt in on tax return
Can non-residents use it?✅ Yes
When to fileAnnual Italian return (Modello 730/Unico), due Oct/Nov each year
💡 You must register a CIN (Codice Identificativo Nazionale) — Italy's national short-let registration system introduced in 2024. Without this, you cannot legally advertise the property on Airbnb/Booking.com in Italy.

🇮🇹 Italian Property Ownership Taxes

IMU (property ownership tax)~€300–700/yr
Applies toNon-resident second homes
Rate0.86%–1.14% of revalued cadastral value
Cadastral value~15–25% of market price in south Italy
TARI (waste/bins tax)€150–400/yr
Payment scheduleIMU: June & December

🇬🇧 UK Tax Obligations (Higher Rate, 40%)

HMRC Self Assessment Required

As a UK resident, you must declare worldwide income. Italian rental income goes on SA106 (Foreign pages of your Self Assessment return). Italy has primary taxing rights under the 1988 UK-Italy Double Taxation Agreement.

Declaration formSA106 (supplementary foreign income pages)
UK tax rate (your band)40% on net rental profit
Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)Credit for Italian tax paid (up to UK rate)
Net additional UK tax~19% (40% − 21% FTC)
Currency conversionDeclare in £ using HMRC exchange rates
Italian residence certificateGet from HMRC online to claim DTA relief in Italy

🇬🇧 UK Capital Gains Tax (when you sell)

Italy CGT rate26% on capital gain
UK CGT rate (higher rate)24% on residential gains (from Oct 2024)
DTA reliefCredit for Italian CGT against UK CGT
Annual CGT allowance (UK)£3,000 (2024/25 onwards)
Italy exemptionNo CGT if held 5+ years (for natural persons)
✅ Hold the property for 5+ years in Italy and the Italian CGT of 26% is waived. Only UK CGT then applies at 24%, with your £3,000 annual allowance.
